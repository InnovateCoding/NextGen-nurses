Assessment of a Critically ill Patient

1. General consideration for assessment of a Critically ill Patient

A systematic approach and use of checklists can improve outcomes. Use of ‘FAST HUGS-BID’ should be incorporated in patient assessment. Best evidence and current ‘care bundles’ should be incorporated. Good communication and documentation are vital. During initial assessment and in cases of emergencies, airway, breathing, circulation, disability and exposure (ABCDE) approach is appropriate. Head-to-toe assessment approach is important for scheduled and subsequent examinations.

2. Principles of Patient Assessment in Intensive Care Unit

History

Avoid focusing intently on monitors and charts. Patients are important! Don’t ignore them!! Traditional wisdom that 90% of the diagnosis is in the history is equally applicable to ICU patients. If awake, patients can explain their problem, making identification of problems easier. Sometimes, patients might be awake only for a short time before deterioration. So, timely history taking is vital sometimes. Detailed history must be taken at admission only after stabilization. Sometimes old documents and referral letters might be the only sources for history. History regarding new issues and problems should be taken at the start of each shift. Information regarding a patient's physiological reserve, their quality of life, and their attitude towards treatment is equally important.

Physical Examination (Head-to-Toe Approach)

A systematic approach must be used and a ‘Head-to-Toe’ system is appropriate. The type and frequency of monitoring should be based on the underlying disease process, the physiological reserve of the patient, and the degree of clinical suspicion. Height or length of the patient should be measured and documented. Predicted body weight (PBW) should be calculated and recorded. If possible, actual body weight (ABW) of the patient should be measured and documented daily. Try to avoid making assumptions about what other medical staff has done. Perform your own examination.

icu tools

Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS): Frequency of examination might depend upon the disease condition. In most instances, it is monitored hourly. If the patient is sedated, for example to control intra cranial pressure (ICP) or in severe acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS), GCS should be assessed only after stopping the sedatives. If the patient has no neurological issues, GCS can be assessed only 1-2 times a day to promote sleep. Full Outline of UnResponsive Score (FOUR) score can be used alternative to GCS, if decided by clinical team. Pupils: Size and reaction should be performed and recorded. It should be assessed and recorded every hour in patients who are sedated or have any neurological issues. In patients, with no neurological issues, assessment frequency can be changed. Pronator Drift: Pronator drift should be assessed every hour in patients with neurological issues or patients who might develop neurological issues. Grip, Motor Power and Sensory examination to touch: Should be done every hour in patients with neurological issues or patients who might develop neurological issues. A full cranial and peripheral nerve Examination: Should be performed once daily when indicated. e.g., Guillain Barre Syndrome (GBS) Sedation Score (Richmond Agitation Sedation Scale (RASS): Target and achieved RASS, both should be recorded. Any change in RASS should also be recorded. Pain Score: Numeric Rating Scale (NRS)/Visual Analogue Scale (VAS)/Critical Care Pain Observation Tool (CPOT) should be assessed and recorded every hour in all patients receiving analgesics. If there are no issues of pain, assessment frequency can be changed. Delirium: Confusion Assessment method for the ICU (CAM-ICU) Score should be assessed once every 6 hours or more frequently if indicated. Sleep Pattern: Should be observed and recorded. Seizure episodes: Record if any seizure occurs. The type of seizure should be recorded. Intracranial Pressure (ICP) Monitoring: Should be measured continuously or as indicated. Cerebral Perfusion Pressure (CPP) should be recorded. Monitor External Ventricular Drain (EVD) in terms of level/height, open/clamped, drain volume and characteristics.

Respiratory System and Ventilation

Airway: Self maintained or endotracheal intubated or tracheostomized. Endotracheal Tube: Record Size/ length fixed/ position. FiO 2 and mode of O 2 delivery should be assessed and recorded. Pattern of Respiration: Assess for respiratory distress: regular/irregular respiration; labored breathing or not; accessory muscles used or not at least 6 hourly. Chest Auscultation: Lungs should be auscultated for air entry and added sounds (wheeze or crackles) every hour in all mechanically ventilated patients and at least 6 hourly in non-ventilated patients. Mechanical Ventilation: Mode and Settings should be assessed and recorded. Plateau pressure/Peak airway pressure/Driving pressure/Tidal Volume/Minute ventilation/I:E ratio should be monitored and recorded every hour.

Monitoring:

SpO2: Should be monitored continuously and recorded every hour. Respiratory Rate: Should be measured and recorded every hour. PaO2/FiO 2 Ratio: Should be measured at least once daily in mechanically ventilated patients or more frequently as indicated. PaCO2: Should be measured at least once daily in mechanically ventilated patients or more frequently as indicated. EtCO2: Should be monitored continuously in all mechanically ventilated patients. In non-intubated patient, cough, secretion load and swallow function should be assessed every 6 hours. In a mechanically ventilated patient: Assess oral and tube secretion every hour or as indicated. Assess and maintain Cuff pressure every hour. Chest Physiotherapy: Record if done. Chest Drain: Monitor and record-Site, drain Volume, column movement and bubbling. Note any surgical emphysema.

Cardiovascular System

Heart Rate/ Pulse Rate: Should be monitored continuously and recorded every hour. Electrocardiogram (ECG): Rhythm, pacing, arrhythmias should be monitored continuously. Blood Pressure: Monitor Invasive or Non-invasive methods of recording. Systolic Blood Pressure (SBP)/Diastolic Blood Pressure (DBP)/Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP): Should be measured and recorded every hour. The frequency can be changed to less frequent once the patient is stable and ready for transfer-out in the next 24-48 hours. Support for Blood Pressure: Vasopressors and required doses should be recorded. Peripheral perfusion: Capillary refill time should be examined and recorded as Brisk, Sluggish or Absent at least 6 hourly or more frequently if indicated. Peripheral pulses: Dorsalis Pedis and Radial pulses should be examined at least 6 hourly or more frequently if indicated. Peripheral edema: Should be examined at least once in a shift. Grading of edema should be done. Jugular Venous Pressure (JVP): Raised JVP should be looked for at least once a day. Mucosal dryness: Mucus membrane should be examined for dryness at least every 6 hours. Temperature: Should be measured and recorded hourly. Auscultation of the heart: Should be done at least once a day. Hemodynamic Monitoring (when indicated and available): Central Venous Pressure (CVP)/Right Atrial Pressure (RAP). Cardiac Output (CO)/Cardiac Index (CI). Systemic Vascular Resistance (SVR)/Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (PVR). Left Atrial Pressure (LAP)/Pulmonary Capillary Wedge Pressure (PCWP). Pulse Pressure Variation (PPV).

Gastrointestinal System and Nutrition

Full Abdominal examination: Should be done at least once daily. Surgical Drains: Volume and drain characteristics should be monitored continuously. The drain amount should be recorded once a day or more frequently if indicated. Bowel Sound: Assess and record every 6 hours. Abdominal Girth: Measure and record every 6 hourly when indicated. Intra-Abdominal Pressure (IAP): Measure and record every 6 hourly when indicated. Bowel Movement and Stool characteristics: All bowel movements and date and time of last bowel movement should be recorded. Nasogastric/Orogastric Tube: Confirmation of position should be done daily. Monitor drain volume and character if used for drainage

Nutrition:

Daily Calorie and protein target should be calculated and recorded Last 24-hour calorie and protein intake should be calculated and recorded. Route and volume of feeding should be assessed and recorded.

Social Issues

يجب تحديد Surrogate Decision Maker (SDM) وهو من ينوب عن المريض في اتخاذ القرار. توثيق أهداف الرعاية أو التوجيهات المسبقة (Advance Directives) إن وجدت. تقييم مدى الهشاشة أو الضعف الجسدي (Frailty) ضروري.

Best Practices with FAST HUGS BID

Feeding: التأكد من تقييم الحالة الغذائية وتلبية احتياجات المريض. Analgesia: تقييم الألم وتقديم مسكنات مناسبة. Sedation: مراقبة درجة التسكين وتجنب المبالغة. يجب التفكير في إجراء فترات إيقاف التسكين اليومية. Thromboprophylaxis: إعطاء جرعة وقائية من الهيبارين تحت الجلد لجميع المرضى إن لم يكن هناك مانع. استخدام الأجهزة الضاغطة في حال وجود موانع. Head of Bed (HOB): يجب رفع رأس السرير بزاوية من 30-45 درجة. Ulcer Prophylaxis: يجب إعطاء وقاية من قرح المعدة للمرضى على جهاز التنفس الصناعي. Glycemic Control: الحفاظ على مستوى الجلوكوز <180 mg/dL وفق بروتوكول ICU. Spontaneous Breathing Trial: تجربة التنفس العفوي. Bowel Care: العناية بوظائف الإخراج. Indwelling Catheters: تقييم ضرورة استمرار استخدام القساطر يوميًا. De-escalation of Antibiotics: مراجعة إمكانية تقليل أو إيقاف المضادات الحيوية حسب حالة المريض. Mobilization: استخدام أدوات تقييم مثل S5Q لتحديد قدرة المريض على الحركة والنشاط البدني. Severity Score of Illness: حساب مؤشرات شدة المرض مثل: TropICS SOFA APACHE II ICH Score وغيرها حسب الحالة.

Renal, Fluids and Metabolic

  • Urine Output: يجب قياس وتسجيل إخراج البول كل ساعة في الحالات التي تستدعي ذلك
  • Foley Catheter: يجب تقييم مدة تركيب القسطرة البولية وضرورة استمرارها بشكل يومي.
  • يجب حساب التوازن اليومي والتراكمي للسوائل (كمية الداخل والخارج).
  • Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT): يجب تسجيل كمية السوائل المسحوبة (Ultrafiltrate) ومدة العلاج.
  • يجب مراقبة مستوى الجلوكوز في الدم وفقًا للبروتوكول المحدد.

Skin and Extremities

حساب Braden Score يوميًا لتقييم خطر حدوث تقرحات الفراش.

  • البحث عن علامات تجلط الأوردة العميقة DVT مثل التورم أحادي الجانب، الألم، السخونة، والاحمرار.
  • يجب فحص أي تورم أو جرح أو إفرازات أو غيارات
  • في حال وجود علامات جراحية أو ندوب أو قرح، يجب تقييمها وتوثيقها.

Lines and Catheters

  • تقييم وتسجيل تاريخ الإدخال، وتغيير الغيارات، وكفاءة عمل:
  • Central Venous Catheter
  • Arterial line
  • Peripherally Inserted Central Catheter (PICC)
  • Dialysis Catheter
  • Endotracheal Tube / Tracheostomy Tube
  • Foley Catheter
  • Orogastric / Nasogastric / PEG Tube
  • Peripheral Intravenous lines

Infectious Diseases

  • تقييم وتوثيق أي علامات للعدوى مثل الحمى، الاحمرار الموضعي، التورم، الألم.
  • مراجعة المؤشرات المخبرية للعدوى مثل:
  • WBC
  • CRP
  • Gram Stain (GS)
  • Culture Sensitivity (CS)
  • PCR

Investigations

  • استخدام POCUS (Point of Care Ultrasound) عند الحاجة
  • مراجعة التحاليل الطبية والصور الشعاعية.
  • متابعة نتائج الفحوصات المنتظرة.
