CVP Measurement Procedure

1. Assess the patient's respiratory and cardiac status.

  • 1. Assess the patient's respiratory and cardiac status.
    Rationale: To ensure the patient is stable enough for CVP measurement and to detect any baseline abnormalities.
  • 2. Wash hands and wear gloves.
    Rationale: To prevent infection and maintain aseptic technique.
  • 3. Select the appropriate CVC lumen.
    Rationale: To use the correct port designated for pressure monitoring, avoiding medication or nutrition ports.
  • 4. Begin the IV solution such as normal saline or Ringer's lactate.
    Rationale: To ensure patency of the catheter and facilitate accurate pressure transmission.
  • 5. Close the stopcock in the direction of the patient.
    Rationale: To isolate the patient from the IV solution line for accurate CVP measurement.

وضعية المريض وقراءة القياس

  • 6. Allow the solution to pass via the manometer line
    Rationale: To prime the manometer with fluid, preventing air bubbles that could affect readings.
  • 7. Check the patency of the catheter and lumen through flush back or using a syringe.
    Rationale: To confirm that the catheter is not blocked and is functioning correctly.
  • 8. Position the patient appropriately.
    Rationale: Proper positioning ensures consistent and accurate CVP measurements.
  • 9. Position the manometer according to patient position:
    • Flat: Mid-axillary line at the fifth intercostal space.
    • Fowler’s or Semi-Fowler’s: Mid-axillary line at the second intercostal space.
    Rationale: Aligns the manometer with the right atrium for standardization of CVP readings.
  • 10. Close the stopcock in the direction of the solution.
    Rationale: To direct the pressure line solely to the manometer for measurement.

إنهاء الإجراء والتوثيق

  • 11. Take the CVP reading when fluid stops fluctuating (Normal: 5-15 cmH₂O).
    Rationale: A steady fluid column gives an accurate measurement of central venous pressure.
  • 12. Begin IV flush with normal saline or heparinized saline (if not contraindicated).
    Rationale: To maintain catheter patency and prevent clot formation.
  • 13. Place the patient in a comfortable position.
    Rationale: Enhances patient comfort and reduces anxiety post-procedure.
  • 14. Remove gloves and wash hands.
    Rationale: To maintain hygiene and prevent spread of infection.
  • 15. Document date, time, CVP reading, and catheter insertion site.
    Rationale: Accurate documentation is essential for ongoing patient care and monitoring trends.
