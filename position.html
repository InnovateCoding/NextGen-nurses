Patient Positions

This section introduces essential patient positions used in nursing care to ensure patient comfort, safety, and effective medical procedures.

Fowler’s Position (الوضع شبه الجالس)

The patient is seated at a 45-60 degree angle. It helps improve breathing and is commonly used for feeding and respiratory issues.

✅ الاستخدامات (Uses):

  • تسهيل تناول الطعام وتقليل خطر الشفط الرئوي.و تحسين التنفس
  • إجراء الأشعة السينية (X-ray) على السرير.
  • إدخال أنابيب التغذية الأنفية المعدية (NG tube).
  • العناية بعد العمليات الجراحية في البطن أو الرئتين.
  • يُستخدم أثناء الراحة، بعض الجراحات، وعمليات التصوير المقطعي (CT scan).

⚠️ المخاطر (Risks):

  • تقرحات الضغط نتيجة زيادة الضغط على عظام الأرداف.
  • خطر الشفط الرئوي إذا كان المريض فاقداً للوعي.
  • احتمال السقوط إذا لم يُؤمَّن المريض بشكل جيد.
  • غير مناسب لبعض الحالات مثل السمنة المفرطة أو الاستسقاء البطني.

Supine Position

Patient lies flat on their back with arms at the sides. Common and neutral position used in various procedures.

Uses: Surgeries, imaging (CT, MRI), rest, physical exams
Benefits: Easy access to anterior body, neutral spinal alignment
Risks: Pressure ulcers, aspiration if unconscious, reduced lung expansion

Learn More

Prone Position

Patient lies flat on their stomach with head turned to one side. Used for back surgeries and certain respiratory treatments.

Uses: Spinal surgeries, pressure injury prevention, ARDS treatment
Benefits: Improves oxygenation, pressure relief from sacrum
Risks: Neck strain, limited airway access, pressure on abdomen and chest

Learn More

Trendelenburg Position

Patient lies on back with head lower than feet. Table is tilted about 15–30° downward.

Uses: Shock management, central line placement, pelvic surgery
Benefits: Enhances venous return, improves visibility of pelvic organs
Risks: Increased intracranial/intraocular pressure, aspiration risk

Learn More

Lithotomy Position

Patient lies on back with hips and knees flexed, thighs apart, feet in stirrups. Common in gynecological and urological exams.

Uses: Childbirth, pelvic surgery, gynecologic and rectal exams
Benefits: Maximum pelvic access, patient stability
Risks: Nerve injury, circulatory restriction, back discomfort

Learn More

Lateral Position

Patient lies on either right or left side with knees slightly bent. Common for rest, post-operative recovery, and pressure relief.

Uses: Post-surgical recovery, pressure sore prevention, rest
Benefits: Reduces sacral pressure, allows secretion drainage
Risks: Shoulder and hip pressure, spinal misalignment if unsupported

Learn More

Sim's Position

Used primarily for rectal exams, enemas, and perineal procedures. The patient lies on the left side with the left leg straight and the right knee bent. Promotes access and comfort.

Uses: Rectal exams, enemas, perineal care, gynecological procedures, unconscious patient drainage
Benefits: Comfort, optimal access, reduced aspiration risk
Risks: Pressure injuries, nerve damage, venous pooling, reduced lung capacity

Learn More

Left Lateral (Recovery) Position

Patient lies on the left side with the right leg bent and left arm behind. A recovery-safe position used after anesthesia or seizures.

Uses: Recovery after surgery, unconscious patients, post-seizure
Benefits: Promotes drainage, reduces aspiration risk
Risks: Pressure injuries if prolonged, limited mobility
